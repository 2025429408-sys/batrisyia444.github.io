My Hobby

Kamikochi, Japan

The Japanese Alps contain the stunning mountain valley known as Kamikōchi. It is a component of the Chūbu-Sangaku National Park and is renowned for its high mountain peaks, crystal-clear rivers, and breathtaking natural landscape. The Azusa River, which flows through the valley with pristine water, is one of Kamikōchi's most well-known sights. Walking along the river pathways and over the famous Kappa Bridge, which provides an ideal view of the surrounding mountains, especially the Hotaka mountain range, are popular activities for tourists.

Appenzell, Switzerland

Appenzell is a charming little town in northeastern Switzerland. It is well known for its beautiful environment, traditional Swiss culture, and tranquil rural way of life. Each of the two regions, Appenzell Ausserrhoden and Appenzell Innerrhoden, has its own history and natural beauty. Among Appenzell's most attractive features are its colorful village houses, which are decorated with elaborate paints and wood patterns. The town feels quite traditional because it continues to observe a number of customs and cultural practices that have been passed down over the years.

Miyama, Japan

Miyama is a serene rural community in Japan's Kyoto Prefecture. The community known as Kayabuki no Sato is particularly well-known for its traditional thatched-roof homes, or kayabuki. One of the greatest locations in Japan to see what rural life was like in the past is this village. With mountains, rivers, rice fields, and little wooden houses encircled by vegetation, Miyama boasts a serene and natural landscape. People who like to unwind and take in peaceful sights of the countryside will find it ideal since it feels distant from the bustling cities.

Gimbap

Gimbap is a well-known Korean cuisine from South Korea that consists of rice and other ingredients wrapped in gim, or dried seaweed. After that, it is cut into bite-sized pieces, which make it convenient to eat and ideal for on-the-go meals. Eggs, carrots, cucumbers, spinach, pickled radish, and occasionally meat like beef or ham are typical fillings for gimbap. Sesame oil and a small amount of salt are typically used to season the rice, giving it a nutty, light taste.
